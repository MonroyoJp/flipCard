Charles-Augustin de Coulomb
Physics
Electrostatics
Formulated Coulomb's law describing electrostatic force between charges; developed instruments to measure electrostatic phenomena and frictional electricity.
Alessandro Volta
Physics / Electricity
Voltaic Cell
Invented the Voltaic pile (first chemical battery), enabling continuous electric current and founding electrochemistry and power sources.
Hans Christian Ørsted
Physics
Electromagnetism
Discovered that electric currents create magnetic fields (experimental link between electricity and magnetism), initiating the study of electromagnetism.
André-Marie Ampère
Physics
Ampère's Law
Formulated laws describing magnetic effects of electric currents; foundational work for electrodynamics (Ampère's law, units named after him).
Paul Erman
Physics / Geomagnetism
Earth's Magnetism
Performed early measurements of Earth's magnetic field and contributed to geomagnetic observations and instrumentation.
Michael Faraday
Physics
Electromagnetic Induction
Demonstrated that changing magnetic fields induce electric currents; discovered electromagnetic induction, foundations for generators and transformers.
James Clerk Maxwell
Physics
Maxwell's Equations
Unified electricity and magnetism into electromagnetic theory; predicted electromagnetic waves and linked light to electromagnetism.
Heinrich Hertz
Physics
Radio Waves
Experimentally produced and detected electromagnetic waves, confirming Maxwell's predictions and enabling radio communication.
Wilhelm Röntgen
Physics
X-rays
Discovered X-rays (1895), which revolutionized imaging and diagnostic medicine and opened new research in electromagnetic phenomena.
Albert Einstein
Physics
Relativity
Developed special and general relativity, transforming concepts of space, time, mass and energy (E=mc²) and gravity as spacetime curvature.
Ernest Rutherford
Physics
Nuclear Model & Proton
Discovered the nucleus structure via scattering experiments; identified the proton and proposed the nuclear model of the atom.
Wolfgang Pauli
Physics
Pauli Exclusion Principle
Formulated the exclusion principle that governs electron arrangements and the structure of the periodic table and matter stability.
Werner Heisenberg
Physics
Quantum Mechanics
Developed matrix mechanics and the uncertainty principle, central to the formulation of quantum mechanics.
Erwin Schrödinger
Physics
Wave Mechanics
Developed wave mechanics (Schrödinger equation), an equivalent formulation of quantum theory focusing on wavefunctions.
Paul Dirac
Physics
Relativistic Quantum Mechanics
Formulated the Dirac equation combining quantum mechanics and special relativity; predicted antimatter (positron).
James Chadwick
Physics
Neutron
Discovered the neutron (1932), crucial for understanding atomic nuclei and enabling nuclear fission research.
Otto Hahn
Physics / Chemistry
Nuclear Fission
Discovered nuclear fission experimentally; work enabled development of nuclear energy and weapons (shared context with chemistry discoveries).
John Bardeen
Physics
Superconductivity & Transistor
Explained superconductivity (BCS theory co-laureate) and co-invented the transistor — major advances in electronics and condensed matter physics.
Murray Gell-Mann
Physics
Quark Model
Developed the classification scheme for hadrons and proposed quarks as fundamental constituents of matter.
Karl Alexander Müller & Johannes Georg Bednorz
Physics
High-Temperature Superconductors
Discovered superconductivity at higher temperatures in ceramic materials, opening a new research field in superconductors.
Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit
Chemistry / Instrumentation
Mercury Thermometer
Invented a reliable mercury thermometer and developed the Fahrenheit temperature scale used in some countries today.
Benjamin Franklin
Chemistry / Electricity
Electric Charge Conventions
Distinguished positive and negative electric charges and conducted influential experiments on electricity (kite experiment contextually known).
Antoine-Laurent de Lavoisier
Chemistry
Modern Chemistry
Established law of conservation of mass, named oxygen, and performed combustion experiments, transforming chemistry into a quantitative science.
Henry Cavendish
Chemistry / Physics
Hydrogen & Water
Identified 'inflammable air' (hydrogen) and showed that water is produced by combustion of hydrogen with oxygen.
Mikhail Tsvet
Chemistry / Techniques
Paper Chromatography
Developed adsorption chromatography techniques (paper chromatography) for separating plant pigments and mixtures.
Jaroslav Heyrovský
Chemistry / Electrochemistry
Polarography
Invented polarography — an electrochemical method for analyzing substances — and received the Nobel Prize for it.
Phoebus Levene
Chemistry / Biochemistry
DNA Components
Discovered deoxyribose sugar and clarified nucleotide structure, contributing to understanding DNA chemistry.
Neil Bartlett
Chemistry
Noble Gas Compounds
Showed that noble gases can form compounds (first xenon compound), overturning the idea that inert gases were entirely unreactive.
Hugo de Vries
Biology / Genetics
Mutation Theory
Proposed the concept of mutations as a source of variation (alongside Mendelian rediscoverers); contributed to early genetics.
Alan Guth
Cosmology
Inflationary Universe
Proposed the inflationary universe theory explaining a rapid expansion phase in the early universe.
Robert Hooke
Biology
Cell Discovery
Observed cork structures and coined the term “cell” when viewing plant tissue under a microscope.
Anton van Leeuwenhoek
Biology / Microbiology
Microorganisms
Built powerful single-lens microscopes and was the first to observe bacteria and other microscopic life (microbiology foundations).
Carolus Linnaeus
Biology / Taxonomy
Binomial Nomenclature
Developed the system of classifying species using two-part Latin names (genus + species) still used in taxonomy.
Mary Anning
Paleontology / Biology
Fossil Discoveries
Discovered important fossil specimens including Ichthyosaur fossils, contributing to early paleontology and extinction understanding.
Georges Cuvier
Biology / Comparative Anatomy
Comparative Anatomy
Established comparative anatomy and paleontology; argued for species extinction and studied fossil evidence scientifically.
Robert Brown
Biology / Botany
Cell Nucleus
Discovered the cell nucleus; contributed to cell biology and plant cell observations.
Hugo de Vries, Carl Correns & Erich Tschermak
Biology / Genetics
Rediscovery of Mendel
Independent rediscovery and confirmation of Mendel's laws of inheritance around 1900, forming modern genetics foundations.
Dmitry Ivanovsky & Martinus Beijerinck
Biology / Virology
Discovery of Viruses
Ivanovsky's tobacco mosaic studies and Beijerinck's work led to concept of viruses as infectious agents smaller than bacteria.
Martin Cline
Biology / Genetic Engineering
Gene Transfer
Performed early experiments transferring functional genes into mice; involved in early gene therapy research (historical context).
Allan Wilson & Russell Higuchi
Biology / Molecular Genetics
Ancient DNA & Cloning Insights
Pioneering work in molecular evolution and ancient DNA; contributed to cloning and molecular techniques linked to extinct species studies.
Ian Wilmut
Biology / Cloning
Dolly the Sheep
Led team that cloned Dolly via somatic cell nuclear transfer — first mammal cloned from an adult somatic cell (1996).
Rudolf Jaenisch
Biology / Genetics
Genetic Modification in Mammals
Introduced foreign DNA into mouse embryos, pioneering transgenic animal models for genetic research and disease study.
Leon Philippe Teisserenc de Bort
Earth Science / Meteorology
Stratosphere
Discovered and characterized the stratosphere — distinct atmospheric layer above the troposphere with stable temperature profile.
Andrija Mohorovičić
Geophysics
Moho Discontinuity
Discovered the boundary between Earth's crust and mantle (the "Moho") using seismic wave observations.
Alfred Wegener
Geology
Continental Drift
Proposed continents move over geological time (continental drift), later supported by plate tectonics evidence.
Motonori Matuyama
Geophysics
Geomagnetic Reversals
Presented evidence for periodic reversals of Earth's magnetic field (polarity reversals) important for paleomagnetism.
Charles Francis Richter
Seismology
Richter Scale
Developed the Richter magnitude scale to quantify earthquake sizes (historically influential in seismology).
Harry Hess
Geology / Oceanography
Seafloor Spreading
Proposed seafloor spreading, providing mechanism for continental drift and plate tectonics through mid-ocean ridges.
Walter Alvarez
Earth Science / Geology
Impact Extinction Hypothesis
Proposed that a large impact (asteroid/comet) caused the Cretaceous–Paleogene mass extinction, supported by global iridium layer evidence.
Edwin Hubble
Astronomy
Galaxies & Expansion
Demonstrated other galaxies exist beyond the Milky Way and observed redshift-distance relation indicating the expanding universe.
Clyde Tombaugh
Astronomy
Pluto Discovery
Discovered Pluto (1930) while surveying the outer solar system for trans-Neptunian objects.
Karl Guthe Jansky
Radio Astronomy
Radio Waves from Space
Detected radio emissions from the Milky Way, founding radio astronomy and opening a new window to the cosmos.
Georges Lemaître
Cosmology
Primeval Atom / Big Bang
Published early theoretical model of an expanding universe (primeval atom), a precursor to Big Bang cosmology.
George Gamow, Ralph Alpher & Robert Herman
Cosmology
Big Bang Predictions
Developed nucleosynthesis models and predicted residual cosmic microwave background radiation as part of Big Bang theory refinements.
Jocelyn Bell Burnell
Astronomy
Pulsars
Discovered pulsars—regular radio pulses from rotating neutron stars—transforming understanding of compact stellar remnants.
Neil Armstrong & Buzz Aldrin
Astronomy / Spaceflight
Moon Landing
First humans to walk on the Moon (Apollo 11, 1969), marking a milestone in human space exploration and lunar science.
Fritz Zwicky
Astronomy / Astrophysics
Dark Matter Evidence
Proposed (1930s) that unseen mass (dark matter) is needed to explain galaxy dynamics; early detection of possible dark matter effects.
